# Legal Clause Definitions and Templates

## 1. Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) Clauses

### 1.1 Definition of Confidential Information
**Purpose**: Defines what information is protected under the agreement.

**Standard Language**:
"Confidential Information" means any information, technical data, or know-how, including, but not limited to, that which relates to research, product plans, products, services, customers, customer lists, markets, software, developments, inventions, processes, formulas, technology, designs, drawings, engineering, hardware configuration information, marketing, finances, or other business information disclosed by the Disclosing Party either directly or indirectly in writing, orally, or by drawings or observation.

**Key Elements**:
- Types of information covered
- Exclusions (publicly available, independently developed)
- Marking requirements (if any)

### 1.2 Obligations of Receiving Party
**Purpose**: Establishes how confidential information must be handled.

**Standard Language**:
The Receiving Party agrees to: (a) hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not to disclose the Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent; (c) not to use the Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose; (d) to take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure.

**Key Elements**:
- Confidentiality obligation
- Non-disclosure requirements
- Permitted use limitations
- Security measures

### 1.3 Term and Duration
**Purpose**: Specifies how long confidentiality obligations last.

**Standard Language**:
This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of [X] years from the Effective Date. The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of [Y] years.

**Common Durations**:
- Agreement term: 1-5 years
- Survival period: 2-7 years
- Trade secrets: indefinite

## 2. Service Agreement Clauses

### 2.1 Scope of Services
**Purpose**: Defines exactly what services will be provided.

**Standard Language**:
Provider shall perform the services described in Exhibit A ("Services") in accordance with the terms of this Agreement. Any services not specifically described in Exhibit A are excluded from the scope of this Agreement and shall require a separate written agreement.

**Key Elements**:
- Description of services
- Deliverables
- Acceptance criteria
- Change order process

### 2.2 Payment Terms
**Purpose**: Establishes pricing and payment obligations.

**Standard Language**:
Client shall pay Provider the fees set forth in Exhibit B. Payment is due within [30] days of invoice date. Late payments shall accrue interest at [1.5%] per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

**Key Elements**:
- Fee structure
- Payment schedule
- Late payment penalties
- Expense reimbursement

### 2.3 Term and Termination
**Purpose**: Defines agreement duration and exit conditions.

**Standard Language**:
This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of [X] years. Either party may terminate this Agreement: (a) for convenience upon [90] days written notice; (b) for cause upon [30] days written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within such period.

**Key Elements**:
- Initial term
- Renewal provisions
- Termination for convenience
- Termination for cause
- Effect of termination

## 3. Employment Agreement Clauses

### 3.1 Position and Duties
**Purpose**: Defines the role and responsibilities.

**Standard Language**:
Employee is hired for the position of [Title]. Employee shall perform such duties as are customarily associated with such position and such other duties as may be assigned by the Company. Employee shall devote full business time and attention to the performance of duties.

**Key Elements**:
- Job title
- Reporting structure
- Primary responsibilities
- Exclusivity requirements

### 3.2 Compensation and Benefits
**Purpose**: Establishes total compensation package.

**Standard Language**:
Employee shall receive: (a) base salary of $[X] per year, payable in accordance with Company's standard payroll schedule; (b) eligibility for annual bonus up to [X]% of base salary based on performance; (c) participation in Company benefit plans.

**Key Elements**:
- Base salary
- Bonus structure
- Equity compensation
- Benefits eligibility
- Expense reimbursement

### 3.3 Non-Compete Clause
**Purpose**: Restricts competitive activities after employment.

**Standard Language**:
For a period of [X] months following termination, Employee shall not: (a) engage in any business competitive with Company within [geographic area]; (b) solicit or hire any Company employees; (c) solicit any Company customers or clients.

**Key Elements**:
- Duration (reasonableness varies by jurisdiction)
- Geographic scope
- Activity restrictions
- Consideration provided

### 3.4 Intellectual Property Assignment
**Purpose**: Assigns work product to employer.

**Standard Language**:
Employee agrees that all inventions, discoveries, developments, and work product created during employment relating to Company's business shall be the sole property of Company. Employee hereby assigns all rights in such work product to Company.

**Key Elements**:
- Scope of assignment
- Prior inventions excluded
- Moral rights waiver
- Cooperation obligations

## 4. Lease Agreement Clauses

### 4.1 Premises Description
**Purpose**: Identifies the leased property.

**Standard Language**:
Landlord hereby leases to Tenant, and Tenant hereby leases from Landlord, the premises located at [Address], consisting of approximately [X] square feet of [type] space, as more particularly described in Exhibit A ("Premises").

**Key Elements**:
- Property address
- Square footage
- Common areas included
- Permitted use

### 4.2 Rent and Security Deposit
**Purpose**: Establishes payment obligations.

**Standard Language**:
Tenant shall pay monthly rent of $[X], due on the first day of each month, without demand, deduction, or setoff. Upon execution, Tenant shall deposit $[Y] as a security deposit to be held by Landlord.

**Key Elements**:
- Base rent amount
- Payment due date
- Escalation clauses
- Security deposit terms
- Late fees

### 4.3 Maintenance and Repairs
**Purpose**: Allocates maintenance responsibilities.

**Standard Language**:
Landlord shall maintain the structural elements, roof, and building systems. Tenant shall maintain the interior of the Premises in good condition and repair, including HVAC maintenance, and shall be responsible for repairs caused by Tenant's negligence or misuse.

**Key Elements**:
- Landlord responsibilities
- Tenant responsibilities
- Repair notification process
- Emergency repairs

## 5. Common Boilerplate Clauses

### 5.1 Governing Law
**Purpose**: Specifies which jurisdiction's law applies.

**Standard Language**:
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of [State], without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

### 5.2 Dispute Resolution
**Purpose**: Establishes how disputes will be resolved.

**Standard Language**:
Any dispute arising out of this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation. If mediation is unsuccessful, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of [AAA/JAMS] in [City, State].

**Options**:
- Litigation
- Arbitration
- Mediation
- Multi-tier resolution

### 5.3 Limitation of Liability
**Purpose**: Caps potential damages.

**Standard Language**:
IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES. THE TOTAL LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY SHALL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE [12] MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

**Key Elements**:
- Consequential damage waiver
- Liability cap
- Carve-outs (IP infringement, confidentiality breach)

### 5.4 Force Majeure
**Purpose**: Excuses performance during extraordinary events.

**Standard Language**:
Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, war, terrorism, epidemic, government action, labor disputes, or infrastructure failures, provided the affected party gives prompt notice and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate.

### 5.5 Entire Agreement
**Purpose**: Prevents reliance on external promises.

**Standard Language**:
This Agreement, together with all Exhibits, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, warranties, and agreements between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof.

### 5.6 Assignment
**Purpose**: Controls transfer of rights and obligations.

**Standard Language**:
Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party, except that either party may assign to an affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets.

### 5.7 Severability
**Purpose**: Preserves agreement if parts are invalid.

**Standard Language**:
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable.

## 6. Risk Indicators

### 6.1 High-Risk Clauses to Flag
- Unlimited liability exposure
- Automatic renewal without notice requirements
- One-sided termination rights
- Broad indemnification obligations
- Non-standard governing law provisions
- Waiver of jury trial
- Unreasonable non-compete scope

### 6.2 Missing Protections to Note
- No limitation of liability
- No confidentiality provisions
- No intellectual property assignment
- Missing termination rights
- No dispute resolution mechanism
- Absence of representations and warranties

### 6.3 Negotiation Priority Indicators
- **Must Have**: Essential protections that should not be conceded
- **Should Have**: Important but negotiable terms
- **Nice to Have**: Preferred but not critical terms
- **Walk Away**: Terms that indicate fundamental incompatibility
