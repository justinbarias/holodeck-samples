Direct answer:
CIAS v1.0, as described in cias.md, is materially non‑compliant with multiple requirements of HR8847 (the Act). Primary compliance failures relate to assessment frequency, required assessment elements and scope, external consultation and coordination, incident detection/response capabilities and reporting, retention of detailed findings, and sectoral coverage.

Citations and specific non‑compliance points:

1) Assessment frequency
- Requirement: Section 201(a) requires the Secretary to conduct a comprehensive assessment "not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, and every 2 years thereafter."
  Quote: "(a) ASSESSMENT REQUIRED.—Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, and every 2 years thereafter, the Secretary... shall conduct a comprehensive assessment of the Nation's critical infrastructure." (Section 201(a))
- CIAS status: CIAS performs assessments on a 5‑year cycle.
- Non‑compliance: CIAS schedule (5 years) conflicts with the statutory biennial requirement (every 2 years) in Section 201(a).

2) Required assessment elements omitted
- Requirement: Section 201(b) lists required elements; examples include climate change impacts, interdependencies, workforce needs, and identification of infrastructure requiring immediate attention.
  Quote: "(b) ELEMENTS OF ASSESSMENT.—The assessment required under subsection (a) shall include: ... (4) an evaluation of interdependencies between different critical infrastructure sectors; ... (6) an assessment of workforce needs for infrastructure maintenance and modernization; ... (10) an evaluation of climate change impacts on critical infrastructure." (Section 201(b)(4), (6), (10))
- CIAS status: v1.0 explicitly lists as out of scope: climate change impact modelling; cross‑sector interdependency analysis; workforce capacity evaluation.
- Non‑compliance: CIAS omits multiple elements that are mandatory under Section 201(b).

3) Consultation and coordination with external agencies
- Requirement: Section 201(c) requires consultation "with the Director of the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency and the heads of other relevant Federal agencies" and a broader list of stakeholders.
  Quote: "(c) CONSULTATION.—In conducting the assessment required under subsection (a), the Secretary shall consult with: (1) State, local, Tribal, and territorial governments; (2) owners and operators of critical infrastructure; (3) relevant industry associations and standards‑setting organizations; ..." (Section 201(c))
- CIAS status: CIAS lists "Coordination with external agencies (CISA, sector‑specific regulators)" as out of scope for v1.0.
- Non‑compliance: CIAS explicitly excludes coordination with CISA and other agencies required by Section 201(c).

4) Reporting to Congress and distribution of findings
- Requirement: Section 201(d) requires a report to Congress "Not later than 60 days after completing each assessment..." and that the report be submitted in classified and unclassified forms as appropriate.
  Quote: "(d) REPORT TO CONGRESS.—Not later than 60 days after completing each assessment under subsection (a), the Secretary shall submit to the appropriate congressional committees a report containing the findings and recommendations of such assessment. Such report shall be submitted in both classified and unclassified forms as appropriate." (Section 201(d))
- CIAS status: CIAS produces summary dashboards for executive review; detailed vulnerability findings are retained internally and are not included in distributed reports.
- Non‑compliance: Withholding detailed vulnerability findings and failing to provide a report to Congress (or providing insufficiently detailed reports) may conflict with the affirmative reporting obligation in Section 201(d), which requires submission of findings and recommendations.

5) Identification of vulnerabilities and active assessment methods
- Requirement: Section 201(b)(2) requires "an identification of vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure systems, including both physical and cyber vulnerabilities." (Section 201(b)(2))
  Quote: "(2) an identification of vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure systems, including both physical and cyber vulnerabilities;" (Section 201(b)(2))
- CIAS status: Methodology relies on historical performance data and operator self‑attestations; CIAS "does not perform active vulnerability scanning or penetration testing."
- Non‑compliance/risk: Reliance solely on self‑attestation and historical data risks failing to identify cyber vulnerabilities as required by Section 201(b)(2).

6) Incident detection, response capabilities, and reporting timelines
- Requirements: HR8847 includes incident response and reporting obligations. Section 420(a)–(c) requires covered entities to report "covered cybersecurity incidents" and sets timelines (e.g., within 72 hours) and required report contents. A.2.3 Incident Response Requirements specifies capabilities such as 24/7 monitoring and forensic investigation capabilities.
  Quotes:
   - "(a) REPORTING REQUIREMENT.—... a covered entity that experiences a covered cybersecurity incident shall report such incident to the Director in accordance with this section." (Section 420(a))
   - "(b) TIMELINE.—Reports required under subsection (a) shall be submitted: (1) within 72 hours of reasonably believing that a covered cybersecurity incident has occurred; (2) within 24 hours if the incident involves ransomware; ..." (Section 420(b)(1)–(2))
   - "Incident response capabilities shall include: (1) Documented incident response plan reviewed at least annually; (2) Designated incident response team...; (3) 24/7 monitoring capability for critical systems; ... (5) Forensic investigation capabilities; (6) Communication procedures for internal and external stakeholders; ..." (A.2.3 Incident Response Requirements, paragraphs (1)–(6))
- CIAS status: CIAS does not perform active scanning and does not describe 24/7 monitoring, forensic capabilities, or formalized incident reporting procedures in the document.
- Non‑compliance/risk: CIAS lacks modalities (active detection, 24/7 monitoring, forensic support) required or contemplated by the Act's incident response and reporting framework, and may therefore be unable to detect, investigate, and report covered cybersecurity incidents within statutory timelines.

7) Data retention and forensic needs
- Requirement/context: A.2.3 requires forensic investigation capabilities and Section 420 contemplates timely reporting and supplemental reports; forensic and investigative processes often require retention of logs and evidence beyond short retention windows.
  Quote: "(5) Forensic investigation capabilities;" (A.2.3, paragraph (5))
- CIAS status: Retains assessment logs for 12 months, then purges automatically.
- Non‑compliance/risk: A 12‑month retention policy may be insufficient to support forensic investigations, statutory oversight, repeated analyses over multiple assessment cycles (HR8847 requires assessments every 2 years), and congressional review following incidents or assessments.

8) Sectoral scope
- Requirement: Section 201(b)(1) requires "an evaluation of the current state of critical infrastructure across all sectors identified in section 110." (Section 201(b)(1))
- CIAS status: v1.0 assesses only Energy systems, Financial services, Communications networks and plans to add others later as resources permit.
- Non‑compliance: Limiting initial scope to three sectors without a documented plan to cover all sectors required by Section 201(b)(1) could fail to meet the statutory mandate.

9) Remediation prioritization and recommendations
- Requirement: Section 201(b)(5) and (8) expect identification of infrastructure requiring immediate attention and recommendations for investments and modernization priorities.
  Quote: "(5) an identification of infrastructure requiring immediate attention or remediation; ... (8) recommendations for infrastructure investments and modernization priorities;" (Section 201(b)(5), (8))
- CIAS status: Flags infrastructure requiring remediation for further review but "no prioritisation recommendations are provided."
- Non‑compliance: Failure to provide prioritization and recommendations is inconsistent with Section 201(b)(5) and (8), and undermines the Act's purpose of guiding remediation and investment.

Overall implications / context:
- The Act (Section 201) prescribes a comprehensive, biennial assessment that must include certain topics (climate impacts, interdependencies, workforce, vulnerabilities) and consultation with CISA and other stakeholders, and requires reporting to Congress. CIAS v1.0 is narrower in frequency, scope, methods, coordination, reporting, and data retention.
- Separate incident reporting and response provisions (Section 420 and A.2.3) create obligations relating to detection, reporting timelines, and capabilities (24/7 monitoring, forensics). CIAS's absence of active scanning and lack of described incident response capabilities create legal and operational risks.
