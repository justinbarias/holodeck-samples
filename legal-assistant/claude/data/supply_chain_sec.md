Direct answer

- HR8847 contains two principal provisions that explicitly address supply chain security, plus several related cybersecurity sections that affect implementation and enforcement:
  1) SEC. 411 — Supply Chain Security (detailed supply‑chain risk management requirements, SBOM, and prohibited equipment)
  2) SEC. 231 — Grid Cybersecurity (requires that mandatory bulk electric system standards include “supply chain security”)

Notes and context

- SEC. 411 is the bill’s primary supply‑chain provision. It mandates supply chain risk management programs for owners/operators of critical infrastructure and specifies program elements, including vendor/security evaluation processes, continuous monitoring, contingency planning, and a software bill of materials (SBOM) requirement.
- SEC. 411(b) directs the Director to maintain a list of information and communications technology and services that are prohibited for use in critical infrastructure because they pose unacceptable national security risks.
- SEC. 231 integrates supply chain security into mandatory grid cybersecurity standards for the bulk electric system, meaning supply‑chain requirements will be operationalized through those standards.
- Incident response, coordination, and funding provisions do not add new supply‑chain program requirements but provide supporting authorities, coordination structures, and resources for the supply‑chain rules in SEC. 411 and SEC. 231.
